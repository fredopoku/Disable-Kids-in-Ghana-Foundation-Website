About Us

Championing Every Child's Right to Belong

Founded in 2020 by a coalition of parents, educators, and healthcare workers, the Foundation for Disabled Kids in Ghana (FDKG) exists to break down barriers faced by children with physical, cognitive, and sensory disabilities.

From adaptive classrooms in Accra to mobile health clinics in rural communities, our programs are designed with the belief that inclusion is not a privilege — it is a right.

Dignity First

Every child is treated with respect and compassion.

Community-Driven

We work alongside families, not around them.

Measurable Impact

Every program is tracked, evaluated, and improved.

What We Do

Our Core Programs

Three pillars that form the foundation of everything we do for the children and families we serve.

Inclusive Education

We partner with schools across 10 regions to build accessible classrooms, train teachers in inclusive pedagogy, and provide adaptive learning tools — from Braille kits to sign-language interpreters.

  • Adaptive curriculum development
  • Teacher training workshops
  • School accessibility grants

Healthcare Access

Our mobile health teams bring physiotherapy, occupational therapy, and specialist consultations directly to underserved rural communities where clinics are scarce or inaccessible.

  • Mobile health clinics
  • Prosthetics & assistive devices
  • Mental health support

Skills & Livelihoods

For older youth, we offer vocational training in technology, tailoring, agriculture, and entrepreneurship — creating pathways to economic independence and dignity.

  • Vocational training centres
  • Startup seed grants
  • Mentorship network

Real Lives Changed

Success Stories

Behind every statistic is a child, a family, a community transformed.

Education

Ama's Journey

Kumasi, Ashanti Region

Born with a hearing impairment, Ama was turned away from two schools before FDKG helped her enroll in an inclusive classroom with a sign-language interpreter. Today she is top of her class.

Skills

Kofi's Workshop

Accra, Greater Accra

After losing his leg at age 12, Kofi found purpose in our vocational programme. He completed a tailoring course and now runs his own business employing three others.

Healthcare

Abena's Steps

Tamale, Northern Region

Our mobile health team visited Abena's village and identified her cerebral palsy early. Regular physiotherapy has dramatically improved her mobility and independence.
